Travel

By Plane

For those of you travelling from the States, we are currently looking into a group discount. In the meantime, a great webite for travel deals from the States to the UK is skyscanner.com

By Train

The UK has an extensive rail system. Trains depart from London to Cambridge nearly every hour. There are also excellent connections from Scotland & the North via Peterborough, and regional services from Birmingham & the Midlands, East Anglia & the Northwest.You can check train times and make reservations at the National Rail website.

By Car

From the South & from London, Cambridge is a short trip up the M11 motorway. From the north, the A1 and M1 link to the A14 dual carriageway straight to the city, and from the east and west, the A14 again takes you to Cambridge.

The closest car park to the wedding location is the Grand Arcade.

Location

Cambridge is a beautiful City and there is plenty to see and do. Some of our favorite activities and places to visit are listed below. For more information about the City, we suggest you visit te Visit Cambridge website.

Punting

An absolute must in Cambridge, you will see the famous Cambridge College "Backs" while strolling down the river on a traditional punt.

Tour of the Colleges

There are 31 colleges in Cambridge, each is worth a visit, but if you are low on time then Kings, Trinity, and St. John's are the most popular.

Visit a Local Pub

Our favorites are the Mill, the Green Man, and the Green Dragon, also popular are the Anchor and the Eagle.

Fitzwilliam Museum

The most famous museum in Cambridge offers over half a million works of art for visitors to view.

Botanical Gardens

This garden is 40-acres and displays over 8,000 plant species.

Afternoon Tea

A traditional afternoon tea consists of a pot of tea, scones, sandwiches, and cakes. Our favorite places for tea are Brown's and the Orchard.

Walk to Grantchester

A trip to nearby Grantchester is a great activity since the walk there is only about 20 min and takes you along some beatiful scenery.
